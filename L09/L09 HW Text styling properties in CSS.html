Different Painting Techniques

Oil painting uses pigments mixed with oil (usually linseed). It dries slowly, which lets artists blend colors smoothly and build layers over time. This technique is great for rich textures and detailed, realistic work.

Watercolor involves pigments mixed with water, creating light, transparent effects. It dries quickly and is often used for landscapes and soft, flowing compositions. The paper plays a big role in how the paint looks.

Acrylic paint is fast-drying and water-based. It can mimic both oil and watercolor depending on how it’s used. Artists like it because it’s versatile, durable, and easy to clean up.
