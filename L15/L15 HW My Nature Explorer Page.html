Ras Al Khor Wildlife Sanctuary:

Located right in your backyard, this sanctuary features multiple bird-watching hides where you can spot grey herons, reef herons, and hundreds of flamingos. Access is free, but you typically need to coordinate with the Dubai Municipality for permits or arrange to visit the public hides.

Mushrif National Park:

About a 25 to 30-minute drive from Ras Al Khor, this is a massive urban park featuring the Middle East’s first mountain biking trail, vast Ghaf tree forests, and dedicated walking trails.

The Green Planet:

Located in City Walk (approx. 15 minutes away), this biodome gives you a highly immersive indoor rainforest experience to explore exotic plants and animals.

What is a nature explorer?

A nature explorer is someone driven by curiosity and a deep love for the outdoors. They actively investigate, observe, and learn about the natural world—such as plants, wildlife, and landscapes—usually through hobbies like hiking, birding, photography, or eco-tourism.

What is nature exploring?

Nature Exploration in parenting refers to the practice of engaging children in outdoor activities and experiences that foster their connection with nature. Encouraging kids to explore natural environments, like forests, parks, and gardens, helps promote physical activity, well-being, and environmental awareness.
