Tropical Rainforest Jungle

Tropical rainforest jungles are lush, warm, and highly humid ecosystems located near the equator. They feature high annual rainfall and support a vast percentage of the world's plant and animal species.

Seasonal Tropical Jungle

Seasonal Tropical Jungles, also known as Tropical Dry Forests, thrive in regions with severe 3- to 8-month dry seasons and annual rainfall ranging from 250 to 2,000 mm.

Temperate Jungle

A temperate jungle, commonly known as a temperate rainforest, is a lush, high-precipitation forest found in middle latitudes (usually between 40° and 60°).
